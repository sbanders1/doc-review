**PROPOSITION:** The NewCo board of directors breached its enhanced
*Revlon* duties in connection with the sale of NewCo to AcquireCorp
Holdings, LLC, and the certified class is entitled to damages.

1.  *Revlon* duties were triggered because the transaction effected a
    change of control.

    1.  The merger was a sale of control for cash consideration.

        1.  Merger Agreement Section 2.1 fixes consideration at \$42.00
            per share, all cash.
        2.  Pre-signing, no holder owned more than 5 percent of NewCo
            voting stock; public float was 92 percent.
        3.  Post-closing, NewCo will be a wholly-owned subsidiary of
            AcquireCorp Holdings, LLC.

    2.  The *QVC / Time-Warner* exception does not apply.

        1.  There was no pre-existing controlling stockholder.
        2.  The stock-for-stock alternative was rejected at the
            September 22, 2025 board meeting (Minutes at 14).

2.  The board failed to obtain the highest reasonably available price.

    1.  Final consideration regressed materially from preliminary
        indications.

        1.  AcquireCorp’s indication of interest dated August 12, 2025
            proposed \$48 to \$52 per share.
        2.  Final agreed price was \$42.00 per share, a 16 percent
            reduction off the IOI midpoint.
        3.  No material adverse change occurred between the IOI and
            signing (Disclosure Schedule 3.8 lists none).

    2.  A higher topping bidder was rebuffed without analysis.

        1.  Strategic Bidder X submitted an unsolicited written proposal
            at \$46 per share on September 30, 2025.
        2.  Board minutes of October 5, 2025 reject Bidder X in two
            paragraphs, citing only “execution risk.”
        3.  No financial analysis of Bidder X’s offer was distributed to
            the board (production set NEWCO-BD-00041).

    3.  The Goldman fairness opinion is not a reliable price benchmark.

        1.  Goldman’s DCF used an 11.0 percent WACC; its 2024 opinion in
            *Project Aria* used 9.0 percent for the same SIC code.
        2.  A 200 basis point WACC differential reduces implied DCF
            value by approximately 18 percent on NewCo’s projection set.
        3.  Goldman’s \$35 million fee was 95 percent contingent on
            closing (Engagement Letter Section 4(b)).
        4.  Goldman did not run a leveraged buyout or ability-to-pay
            analysis, despite AcquireCorp being a private equity
            sponsor.

3.  The board’s process was conflicted and inadequate.

<!-- -->

    A.  The CEO had material undisclosed financial conflicts.

        1.  The CEO executed an employment agreement with AcquireCorp on October 12, 2025, eleven days before signing.
        2.  Terms include a \$15 million signing bonus and three-year guaranteed compensation of \$4.5 million per year.
        3.  The CEO will roll over 25 percent of his NewCo equity into AcquireCorp Holdings (Merger Agreement Schedule 4.7).
        4.  The conflict was not disclosed to the full board until October 20, 2025.

    B.  The lead independent director was not independent of the buyer.

        1.  Lead Director J. Smith sits on the boards of two AcquireCorp portfolio companies.
        2.  Smith's family office committed \$50 million to AcquireCorp Fund IV, 2024 vintage.
        3.  Smith's Fund IV investment is not disclosed in the Schedule 14A proxy.

    C.  The special committee was formed late and was not functionally independent.

        1.  The committee was constituted on September 14, 2025, after price had been negotiated to \$43 per share.
        2.  Two of three committee members were recruited to the NewCo board by the CEO in 2022 to 2023.
        3.  The committee retained no independent financial advisor; it relied on Goldman.

    D.  Deal protections were preclusive in combination.

        1.  Termination fee of 4.0 percent of equity value, against an industry median for comparable deals of 2.8 percent.
        2.  Five-business-day matching rights (Merger Agreement Section 6.4(d)).
        3.  Force-the-vote provision (Section 7.4) eliminates the fiduciary out short of a superior proposal.
        4.  No-shop with narrow Superior Proposal carve-out (Section 6.3).

    E.  The post-signing go-shop was illusory.

        1.  The go-shop window was 25 calendar days, against an industry norm of 30 to 45.
        2.  Goldman ran the go-shop on the same contingent-fee economics.
        3.  Outreach was limited to 8 strategic buyers; no financial sponsors were contacted.
        4.  Zero topping bids emerged before the December 15, 2025 deadline.

4.  Damages are calculable and material to the class.

    1.  Fair value materially exceeds the merger consideration.

        1.  Plaintiff’s expert unconflicted DCF yields \$54.50 per
            share.
        2.  Selected-transactions analysis (12 comparables, 2022
            to 2025) yields \$51.00 per share at the median EV/EBITDA
            multiple.
        3.  Unaffected-price plus comparable-premium analysis yields
            \$50.00 per share.

    2.  Class composition is determinable.

        1.  145.0 million shares were outstanding at the record date.
        2.  12.0 million shares held by the CEO and other rollover or
            affiliated holders are excluded from the class.
        3.  Class size: 133.0 million shares.

    3.  Aggregate quantum.

        1.  Per-share shortfall at the \$52.00 midpoint of plaintiff’s
            range: \$10.00.
        2.  Aggregate class damages: approximately \$1.33 billion before
            pre-judgment interest.
        3.  Pre-judgment interest at the Delaware legal rate (5 percent
            over the Federal Reserve discount rate, compounded
            quarterly) from closing through judgment.
